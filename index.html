What do these mean?

Realized P&L — Actual profit or loss from positions you've closed (sold out of). Excludes positions you still hold (those are "unrealized") and excludes fees.

Win Rate — The share of your closed trades that made money. 60% means 6 of every 10 trades were profitable. On its own it can mislead: many small wins and a few big losses can still lose money.

Profit Factor — Total dollars won ÷ total dollars lost. Above 1.0 means your winners outweigh your losers in dollar terms; 2.0 means you earn $2 for every $1 you lose. It captures size, not just frequency.

Expectancy — Your average result per trade (total P&L ÷ number of closed trades). Positive means the typical trade makes money. It rolls win rate and win/loss size into one number — your "edge per trade."
